Digital Safety and Privacy

Digital safety and privacy are crucial aspects of using online platforms for health information. In Botswana, ensuring the security and confidentiality of users' data is a priority for digital health initiatives.As more people use websites and mobile applications to access HIV/AIDS information and support, it is important to understand how to stay safe online and protect personal health details.

Safe Internet practices

Users should always follow safe internet practices when accessing health information online. This includes using trusted websites, avoiding sharing personal details on public platforms, and being cautious of suspicious links or messages. Strong passwords and secure connections also help protect users from online threats.

Protecting Health Information & Cybersecurity

Protecting personal health information is important to prevent misuse and cyberbullying. Sharing sensitive information online can expose individuals to negative comments, stigma, or harassment. Users should be mindful of what they share and report harmful behaviour. Promoting respectful communication online helps create a safer digital environment for everyone.

Digital Laws & Protection

Botswana has laws and policies in place to protect individuals’ personal data and ensure responsible use of digital platforms. These regulations help safeguard user information and promote ethical behaviour online. Understanding these protections helps users feel more confident when accessing digital health services.
