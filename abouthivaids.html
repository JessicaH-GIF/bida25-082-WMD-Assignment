About HIV/AIDS

The purpose of this page is to educate young Batswana about the basic science and understanding of HIV/AIDS. It provides clear information on what HIV/AIDS is, how it spreads, methods of prevention, and available treatment options. The content is supported with simple explanations and visual graphics to make learning easier and more engaging.

What is HIV/AIDS?

This section explains that HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that weakens the immune system, while AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) is the advanced stage of untreated HIV infection.

How HIV Spreads AND Its Progression

HIV is transmitted through specific body fluids such as blood, unprotected sexual contact, and sharing of contaminated needles. It does NOT spread through casual contact like hugging or sharing food.

If HIV is not treated, it weakens the immune system over time and can progress to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome). AIDS is the most advanced stage of HIV infection, where the body becomes very vulnerable to infections and diseases due to a severely weakened immune system.

Prevention, Treatment & Living with HIV/AIDS

HIV can be prevented through safe practices such as using protection during sexual contact, avoiding sharing sharp objects, and going for regular testing. Education and awareness also play a major role in reducing new infections.

Although there is no cure for HIV, Antiretroviral Therapy (ART) allows people living with HIV to manage the virus effectively. With proper treatment and support, individuals can live long, healthy, and productive lives. Technology also supports treatment through health apps, reminders, and online counselling services that improve access to care and information.

HIV/AIDS Statistics in Botswana

Botswana is one of the countries most affected by HIV/AIDS in the world. However, it has also made significant progress in testing, treatment, and prevention over the years. A large percentage of adults living with HIV are aware of their status and are receiving treatment through national health programmes.

Current estimates show that HIV prevalence among adults remains high, but ongoing government and health initiatives have helped reduce new infections and improve access to care. Botswana continues to strengthen awareness campaigns, testing services, and treatment programmes to achieve better health outcomes for its population.
